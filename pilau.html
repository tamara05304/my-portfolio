Sweet Pilau

Traditional spiced rice with scientifically optimized cooking methods

Print Recipe Share

Ingredients

Pilau Masala (Spice Mix):

  • 1 tsp cloves
  • 2 tbsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tbsp black peppercorn
  • 12 pods cardamom
  • 1 stick cinnamon

Rice Pilau:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 5 medium potatoes (quartered)
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 red onions (thinly sliced)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp ginger (minced)
  • 1 serrano chile (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp pilau masala
  • 2 beef stock cubes
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • 1 lb beef sirloin (cubed)
  • 3 Roma tomatoes (diced)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste

Method

Step 1: Make the Pilau Masala

Dry roast cumin, black pepper, cinnamon, cardamom and cloves in a pan until fragrant. Blend to powder (coffee grinder works!). Use 1-1½ tbsp for recipe. Store extra in airtight container (lasts 3 months).

Step 2: Caramelize Onions

Heat oil in pot, fry onions 10-15 mins until golden brown (don't burn!). Add garlic, ginger, chile - cook 1-2 mins until your kitchen smells AMAZING.

Step 3: Brown the Beef

Add beef, pilau masala, stock cubes, bay leaves, cilantro, and salt. Cook 8-10 mins until meat is browned and caramelized (this is where the flavor comes from!).

Step 4: Tomato Time

Add tomatoes, cook 4-5 mins until they break down. Stir in potatoes and water, boil for 10 mins.

Step 5: Rice Finish

Add rice, cover TIGHTLY with foil (this is the secret!), then lid. Cook on low 20 mins until liquid is absorbed. Fluff with fork and serve with kachumbari.

The Science Behind This Recipe

  • Dry roasting spices releases essential oils that enhance flavor and aroma
  • Caramelizing onions creates Maillard reactions that develop deep umami flavors
  • The tight foil seal creates a steamy environment for perfect rice texture
  • Cumin and cardamom contain antimicrobial compounds that may aid digestion
  • Slow cooking allows collagen in beef to break down into gelatin for tenderness
